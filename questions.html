  1. What is the Highway Code?

    • It is a booklet checked and passed by the parliament that provides guidelines for road users in Kenya

  2. What is a vehicle?

    • It is an automobile power driven machine

  3. What is a lane?

    • It is a path to be followed by the vehicle

  4. Who is a pedestrian?

    • Road users on foot

  5. What is the rule of the road in Kenya?

    • Always keep left unless overtaking

  6. Which side do you overtake from in Kenya?

    • From the right side

  7. Name three documents which must be valid before you are allowed to drive?

    • Valid driving license
    • Valid vehicle inspection certificate
    • Valid insurance certificate

  8. Which vehicle have right of way?

    • Ambulance with siren
    • Police car with a siren
    • Fire engine with a siren
    • Presidential escort with siren

  9. What is the maximum driving speed limit in town?

    • It is 50Km/h

  10. What is the maximum driving speed limit on highway?

    • Trailers - 65km/h
    • Pickups/lorries - 80km/h
    • Cars - 110km/h

  11. When are you not allowed to drive despite having the three valid required documents?

    • When you are sick
    • When under the influence of drugs or alcohol
    • When fatigued

  12. What do you look for when driving past stationery vehicle?

    • Opening of doors
    • Pedestrian(s) crossing the road

  13. List 4 important tools you must carry when driving for a safari?

    • Wheel spanner
    • Car jack
    • Spare wheel
    • Survival gear (including...spot light, blanket, water, food, etc.)

  14. State 4 major causes of road accidents in Kenya

    • Over-speeding
    • Reckless/careless driving
    • Overloading
    • Drunkenness
